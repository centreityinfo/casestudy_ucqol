The Challenge

Big Ambitions.
A Gap to Close.

The Covid-19 pandemic exposed a gap between where UAE universities wanted to be and where they actually were on digital learning. Institutions had the ambition, the students, and the mandate — but formal online policies, production infrastructure, and scalable faculty training were inconsistent or absent across the board. The opportunity was clear. So was the urgency. Centreity was appointed in 2021 to lead UCQOL — funded by the Abdulla Al Ghurair Foundation — and started the right way: a rigorous 3-month needs assessment across all 9 founding universities. 214 survey questions. Over 600 documents analyzed. 18 stakeholder interviews. A clear evidence base — and a roadmap built to match the scale of the ambition.

Centreity's Role

An Embedded Partner.
Built for Impact.

As lead implementing partner, Centreity designed and ran every phase of UCQOL — from the initial needs assessment through to faculty development, program design, studio builds, accreditation support, and the Communities of Practice now connecting 11+ institutions. The consortium started with 9 founding universities and has continued to grow as results speak for themselves.

Task 1 — Build the Strategy
No two universities were the same. Centreity diagnosed each institution's starting point through the Phase 1 needs assessment, then built bespoke digital roadmaps — covering revenue models, accreditation pathways, and online enrollment strategy — so every decision had evidence behind it.
Task 2 — Develop the People
Faculty capability was the single biggest gap identified across all 9 founding universities. Centreity designed and ran bootcamps, AI workshops, and course design sprints — training 1,200+ faculty and staff to design and deliver online and hybrid learning independently.
Task 3 — Build the Infrastructure
Ideas need production capability behind them. Centreity project-managed the design of 130+ courses and the build of professional recording studios and HyFlex classrooms across campuses — all handed over as university-owned assets on completion.
Task 4 — Embed for the Long Term
The mark of a true implementation partner is what remains after the work is done. Centreity embedded QA frameworks, academic governance workflows, and in-house Learning Experience Designers directly into each university — so the capability, and the results, belong to them permanently.

The UCQOL Model

Four Pillars That Build
Not Borrow

Every UCQOL engagement delivers across four interlocking streams — ensuring institutions build genuine capability, not ongoing dependency on external support.

01
Expert Advisory & Training
  • Tailored strategic consulting on each university's digital transformation challenges
  • Mentoring from leading experts in student-centered learning design
